One moment in the presence of God can change your life forever

The general epistles were written generally, for everyone. Unlike some of the other writings like Colossians or Ephesians

James 1:5

Ideas change, lingos change. Wisdom doesn't change.

The same principles from Adam and Eve in the garden don't change.

You can live a life full of wisdom.

It took 30 years to build the Taj Mahal, and there were 2 of them.

**Anything of value, anything of worth, takes time to build.**

When you take 30 seconds to make a TV dinner, you aren't expecting a filet mignon. But we look at our lives like that, with microwave mentality.

It takes intentionality and effort to make something great and to live a life full of God.

James 3:13-18

Wisdom is not an idea, it is a way of life. An action.

A wise person is coachable and teachable, a person of wisdom.

**The fool is someone who knows what is wise, but chooses to do their own thing, out of pride.**

There is grace when you dont know, havent been taught.

What does it mean to be a wise person of God?

The two cycles, the foolish and the wise:

The foolish-

- Always starts with pride

- Making decisions in isolation. “I can do it on my own.” Making a decision in a vacuum doesnt get you the results you are looking for.

- You make an excuse as to why it is everyone else's fault.

People will look to date without accountability, it doesn't turn out well, and they make excuses.

Proverbs 18:1-2

The wise-

- Always starts with humility. “I don't know it all”

- “Seek out wisdom” you look for it

- Discovers results.

Easy to believe you got here on your own.

1.  Starts with God's Word.

Something powerful about seeing it yourself.

2.  Comes from evaluated experiences.

Just because you get older, doesnt mean you get more wise. You just become more entrenched.

You can pause and evaluate your experiences. Find your blind spots.

**Lessons unlearned = Mistakes repeated**

If over and over, you are having problems, take a step back and evaluate. It probably isn't them, it's you.

“Lord, what are you trying to do in me?”

Even when the other person is wrong, thank God for it.

3.  Wisdom isn't taught, it's transferred.

Do it every day, get up in the morning.

If you are more of a mood person, make it enjoyable. Set the atmosphere, set out candles, some good worship music. Come up with your system that you can get excited about and read your bible every day.

Proverbs 24:3-6 “for by wise guidance you can wage your war, and in abundance of counselors there is your victory”

You arent not getting to the next step because you are lacking information, it is usually a “who” problem. Who is speaking into your life?

“I want to be a leader” it isnt about the what, but who are you receiving from? Who is transferring to you?

His grandfather never was in ministry, but he was just so wise because of how much he studied, he always had the answer Caleb needed
