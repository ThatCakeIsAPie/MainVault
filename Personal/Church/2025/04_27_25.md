Let's Talk Family

In our culture, we are looking for a shortcut in the process.

It's what we care about the most, but where we often feel the least equipped.

When you are single, you are preparing for your future family.

***You need God's help in this area, you cant do it alone. If you try, you will just end up frustrated.***

<u>God's pattern for marriage:</u>

Genesis 2:24 “That is why a man leaves his father and mother and is united with his wife, and they become one flesh.”

Matthew 19:5-6 “...So they are no longer two, but one flesh…”

In affirmation of Genesis, or Jesus

Ephesians 5:31

The marriage relationship is the single most sanctifying relationship you will ever enter into

Marriage is like a tube of toothpaste, it squeezes out what was already in the tube, it doesn't create toothpaste.

If you asked a biblical/trustworthy person for advice, where would they start?

Communication, physical/emotional intimacy, money, expectations, children, career, etc.

Communication is the first one.

When a couple is about to separate, 88% of the time, one of them has no idea it is going on.

Why is marriage the single most sanctifying relationship?

Sanctifying sometimes means difficult, hard, frustrating, growth

You might be able to close a deal, hit a ball, etc. But can you pray with your wife?

Because the marriage relationship is a picture of Jesus and the Church, it comes with ongoing spiritual attack.

*Get God involved*

Because marriage is a covenant, it challenges our desire to negotiate and to treat it like a contract.

Anyone who says marriage is 50/50 has no idea what they are talking about. *It is about all of you*.

Because we primarily think about ourselves, marriage forces us to confront our selfishness.

Because you can't easily or quickly walk away from a marriage, it causes you to deal with your challenges.

*If your kids are the center of your world, you are ruining their future marriage.*

If you are always told that you are amazing, you will search endlessly for that praise without criticism.

Because we start with what we can solve or control, marriage demonstrates our need for God's power.

*You can get wise about the enemies attacks because he uses the same strategies all the time*

How does the Spirit change our marriage?

1.  It leads us to Submit.

Two people submitted to God will come together.

*Quit trying to fix it yourself and submit.*

Ephesians 5:21

2.  It changes how we See It.

The Spirit will give you far more perspective.

3.  It impacts how we Say It.

It isnt what He does in the other person, it is what He does in us.

*If you take away any one thing, it is talk in the Spirit, not in the flesh.*

James 3:5 on the tongue

**Change how you talk. Don't talk in the flesh.**

When you want to say “that”, turn and say what honors God.
