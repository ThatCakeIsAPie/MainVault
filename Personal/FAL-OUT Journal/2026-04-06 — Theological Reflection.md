# 2026-04-06 — Theological Reflection: The "98% Saved" Claim

## The Core Idea
Lyle and his brother are grappling with a claim from a shared mentor: that roughly 98% of Christians are not actually saved. Lyle is looking for biblical precedent to validate this and is currently wrestling with the belief.

## Lyle's Reasoning & Reflection
Lyle isn't reflexively accepting this. He is looking for a biblical grounding for such a radical assertion and finds himself skeptical/unsure of the standard being applied. 

## Questions Raised
1. Is the mentor using the "Narrow Gate" passages (Matthew 7:13-14) as a lens to define salvation by fruit/performance rather than grace?
2. Does this shift the definition of salvation from "justification" (the gift) to "sanctification" (the trajectory)?
3. What is the spiritual impact of this claim? Does it foster genuine holiness or elitism/suspicion of other believers?

## Patterns/Context
This aligns with the theme of "radical ownership" and "high standards" present in Faleth Capital's philosophy, but Lyle is rightly cautious about applying that same high-stakes logic to theological assurance. It's a key example of Lyle balancing external high-performance mentorship with his own scriptural reading.
