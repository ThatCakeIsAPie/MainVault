TAKEAWAYS:

Major:

**Standards and Expectations**- I realized a few weeks before Summit that I let my standards and expectations slip in areas of my life in an effort to protect myself from disappointment. This Summit confirmed my suspicions. The world lowers standards and expectations, because they could not live up to God’s standard. It is not only ok to have standards and expectations for yourself and those in your circle of influence; it is a requirement for success. But not only is it ok and a requirement, *it is the moral thing to do.* Think about it this way: imagine you find a winner out in the marketplace. You invite them to sit down to get connected, they are fired up, arrive early, eager to ask questions, and you arrive with the attitude that they are going to no-show you. Do you think they will be able to feel that? Of course they will. *You will shut them down by your lack of belief.* If you do the right amount of work with the right expectations over a long enough period of time, it will work.

**Polarization**- Much like the previous takeaway, it is important to understand that you will not be everyone’s cup of tea. Your example will challenge others, and not everyone likes a challenge. *But the right ones will rise to it. The winners.* But we are all magnetic in our own way, the only question is this; who are you attracting, who are you repelling, and how strong is that force? Be the man/woman you want to be. Be authentic. You *will* find your people when you start acting like one of theirs.

**It Only Takes 6**- Along the same theme, Larry Winters talks about it every now and then; *begin with the end in mind.* Know what you are trying to accomplish. You only need to find 6 winners who will build it big. The problem; you don’t know who they will be. So you have to treat everyone with love, care and intentionality, believing they will go platinum, believing in them more than they do, understanding that not everyone will live up to it. But at the very least, you aren’t the one putting a lid on them.

**Leaders Are Certain-** I started tearing through the John C Maxwell book from the LTD Bookstore “Leadershift”, and within the first 10 pages, he says something that the conference closely mirrored. Essentially, a leader’s role is to create certainty out of the uncertain. People don’t like uncertainty, it gives them anxiety. But a leader pushes through it anyway, makes a plan, and follows through with it consistently. The more they are consistent, the more others trust them, as they know what to expect from them. That creates certainty, and if you are going somewhere, people will be drawn to you, as following you silences their anxiety. As you speak life into them, tell them you believe in them, give them a clear plan to follow, you provide direction in “a world of endless possibilities”. As Terri Savelle Foy put it: “Your repetition becomes your reputation”

**Two Decisions-** Terri Savelle Foy and Larry Winters both said it. There are two decisions you must make: To never quit. To win. I realize that I made the first decision a long time ago. I thought I made the second decision, as I’ve always believed in my future potential, my ability to grow. But I never gave it a date. I’ve always believed that if I can just stick it out long enough, with enough effort, eventually it will give. I’m determined to win, I don’t care how long it takes. But being real with where I see myself currently, I haven’t seen myself as currently capable in winning in this arena, at this current moment. But hearing it now, I realize I just never truly made the decision to win NOW. There is no such thing as winning in the future. There is only “did I win today?” But like Babe Ruth, that doesn’t necessarily mean home runs, it means swings where I gave it my all.

**Keep Your Dreams in Front of You-** Slightly embarrassed writing this one down lol. The leaders talk about it all the time, but it really struck me how often that train has come through, but never stopped at the station. I would attempt it from time to time, but quickly find myself looking past it to focus on whatever im focusing on. It is easy to lose sight of them. So I just have to make it hard to not look at them. I can change my environment to be a constant reminder of what I’m doing this for. Joe Markiewicz said that there is no such thing as getting tired from working hard if you have a purpose to the work. You only get tired if you are working hard without a purpose. Lexi Caldwell said that people aren’t lazy, they are just uninspired, they forget their dreams because they aren’t in front of them, they are distracted. So as her husband Tony pointed out, you need total immersion. You need to become obsessive about your dreams and immerse yourself in a system that will keep you focused and honed in. If they don’t contribute to your goal, they are taking away from it.

**The Test-** Probably the takeaway that has stuck out in my mind the most, and still has burned a hole in my head, which is this: Joe Markiewicz said that this business is your test. That tickles my Type A personality in just the right way. A test, to prove myself to myself, to show myself what I am really made of. Part of the reason I am so consistent with this business is because of this very reason. This business is my excuse remover. Low barrier to entry, low risk, unlimited upside potential, education, support, running buddies, mentorship to help make it happen, proven products, proven comp plan, proven system, I can go on. If I can’t win here, where would I have a better chance at winning? That is why this business is my test. And I might flunk it a few times, but I will keep taking it until I win.
