# Preamble

The wage and salary model is obsolete.

It was designed for an industrial era that needed interchangeable human parts, not thinking, creative, self-directed people. It pays for time, not value. It punishes efficiency and rewards presence. It severs the natural connection between contribution and reward that humans operated under for tens of thousands of years. And then we wonder why apathy is running rampant — the system gives people no reason to care.

Faleth Capital exists to prove there is a better way.

This is a family office built on a single conviction: that when people own their results, share in the upside and the downside, and are free to work in the way that suits their strengths and their life, they will outperform any system built on control, surveillance, and fixed compensation. Not because they are forced to. Because the structure makes it obvious that their effort matters.

This constitution establishes the principles, structures, and mechanisms by which Faleth Capital operates. It is an open document. We are not big on secrets. Anyone — team member, subsidiary owner, prospective partner, or curious outsider — is welcome to read it, question it, and judge us by whether we live up to it.

This is a document of governing philosophy. It communicates the spirit of the law, not the letter. Detailed mechanics for each area of the system live in their respective framework documents. The principles here are fixed. The parameters in the frameworks are not.

# Article I: Purpose and Mission

## 1.1 — What Faleth Capital Is

Faleth Capital is a family office that owns, funds, and mentors operating businesses across any industry. It is a holding entity, a shared liquidity pool, and a governance body. It is not an employer in the traditional sense. It is an economic ecosystem designed to create business owners, not employees.

## 1.2 — The Mission

**_Create and manage generational wealth._**

That is the mission in its simplest form. Faleth Capital exists to make generational wealth accessible to people who have never had a path to it, and to manage and grow that wealth once they have built it. It does this by building a portfolio of thriving businesses, each operated by people who have genuine ownership over their results, and by proving that the contribution-based model — where compensation is tied to value created rather than time spent — is superior to wages and salaries in every measurable dimension: productivity, retention, innovation, and human dignity.

There is a deeper mission underneath the financial one. Faleth Capital is built on the conviction that human beings flourish most fully when they operate with genuine ownership, genuine freedom, and genuine investment in one another's success. The system is designed to create those conditions — not as a management philosophy, but as a way of life. The wealth is real. The ownership is real. But they are means, not ends. What we are ultimately building is people: people who lead without coercion, who develop others without possessiveness, who build legacies rather than just incomes.

That conviction has roots. The founding philosophy draws from ancient wisdom — the Talmudic model of shared capital, mutual investment, and long time horizons — and from a recognition that the most durable human systems are ones built on genuine care for one another rather than transactional exchange. We do not require that every participant share the same roots. The system is open to anyone who wants to build. But anyone who spends enough time inside it will eventually encounter what makes it tick: a belief that the most powerful force in any organization is people who genuinely want each other to succeed, and that building such an organization is itself a worthy purpose.

## 1.3 — Philosophical Foundation: The One-Third Rule

The financial architecture of Faleth Capital draws from a principle found in the Jewish Talmud: divide your wealth into thirds. One-third in land. One-third in business. One-third in liquid capital.

The Talmud also teaches that families should gather together — traditionally ten — to form a shared liquidity pool. If one member wants to start a business, the pool provides a zero-interest loan, knowing that when the venture succeeds, a portion of its returns will flow back, replenishing and growing the pool for the next person. It is a system of mutual investment built on trust, long time horizons, and the understanding that collective prosperity compounds faster than individual ambition.

Faleth Capital systematizes this wisdom. The family office covers two of the three thirds: business (through its subsidiaries) and liquid capital (through the shared liquidity pool funded by the 50% retention). The third — land, or more broadly, stable long-term assets like real estate and index funds — is the individual's responsibility, built with the passive income the system generates for them.

## 1.4 — The Two Buckets

We encourage every participant to think about their finances in terms of two buckets:

**Bucket One: The X Factor.** This is your safety net. Your "if I get X'd out, my family will be covered" bucket. It is untouched, invested in stable, long-term assets: land, index funds, bonds. It grows quietly and exists so that you never have to make decisions from a place of fear. This is the individual's "land" third.

**Bucket Two: Active Income.** This is everything else — your contribution-based pay, your profit shares, your dividends, your side ventures. Because Bucket One is untouched and growing, Bucket Two gives you freedom to be aggressive: reinvest in your business, mint more shares, fund a new venture, or spend on lifestyle. The security of Bucket One makes the risk tolerance of Bucket Two possible.

The structure of Faleth Capital is designed to help people build both buckets simultaneously. Weekly Layer 2 pay covers living expenses. Quarterly profit shares and dividends build Bucket Two and, when invested wisely, Bucket One. A side network marketing business — made possible by the no-time-clock model — adds an additional passive income stream through overrides on team sales, building Bucket One through the network effect while teaching the participant how to build a business through positive reinforcement rather than negative.

By the time someone is ready to launch their own subsidiary under Faleth Capital, they should have a meaningful safety net in Bucket One and a growing active income in Bucket Two. They are not starting a business out of desperation. They are starting from a position of strength. That is by design.

## 1.5 — Building Networks and Strengthening Them

There are two distinct skills in business that most systems treat as one. The first is building a network from nothing: recruiting, leading, creating relationships, and learning that your income is a direct function of your ability to generate value through other people. The second is integrating into an existing network and making it more valuable: identifying gaps, filling them, strengthening what already works, and expanding the reach of something larger than yourself.

Network marketing teaches the first skill in a way that no other business model does. You start with zero. You have no product to hide behind, no brand recognition to lean on, and — critically — no authority over anyone. The people in your network are volunteers. They joined because they chose to, and they will leave the moment they stop seeing value in staying. You cannot threaten to take away their paycheck, because there is no paycheck to take. You cannot write them up, put them on a performance improvement plan, or hold a promotion over their head. The only tools you have are development, encouragement, and results.

This forces you to learn something that most business leaders never learn, because the traditional model never requires it: how to build people up through purely positive means. You learn to train someone until they can operate without you. You learn to motivate through vision and shared success rather than fear and consequences. You learn to identify someone's strengths, put them in a position to use those strengths, and then get out of the way. You learn that your income is a direct function of how many people you can develop to the point where they no longer need you — and that this is not a loss, but the entire point.

This is the skill that makes the Contribution Framework work. A subsidiary owner operating under this system has no traditional management hierarchy to rely on. Their team is self-organizing. Their Lead is elected, not appointed. Their contributors choose what to work on and when to work. The owner's job is not to control these people. It is to create the conditions under which they thrive. That requires exactly the kind of leadership that network marketing teaches: positive reinforcement, genuine development, and the ability to build people who can function — and eventually lead — without you.

It is not a coincidence that network marketers who start traditional businesses tend to outperform their peers dramatically. They spent years learning how to develop people through positive incentives in an environment with no coercive leverage. When they enter a business context where they also control the structure, the compensation, and the culture, they are operating with a skill set that most first-time business owners spend years acquiring through painful trial and error — if they acquire it at all. We do not include network marketing in the pipeline because it is a good side income, though it is. We include it because it is the most effective training ground we have found for the specific kind of leadership this system demands.

But network marketing has a structural limitation: the hierarchy is permanent. Your position in the tree is your position forever. The upline earns overrides on the downline, the downline can never earn from their upline's business. The person who plugs into an existing network and makes it dramatically better receives no compensation for doing so, outside of building their own network inside said network. Network marketing pays you on expanding the network and making what you expand more valuable, not necessarily on the value you add to the network as a whole.

The [[The Contribution Framework|Contribution Framework]] handles this differently. It rewards both skills. Someone who walks into an existing subsidiary, identifies a gap, and fills it earns contribution points proportional to the value they added. They didn't build the network. They made the network better. The math doesn't care when they arrived or who brought them in. And the Elected Lead system ensures that hierarchy itself is earned and re-earned continuously, not assigned once and frozen in place.

This is not anti-hierarchy. It is anti-static hierarchy. A hierarchy that evolves based on demonstrated value is meritocracy with accountability. A hierarchy that is frozen in place is feudalism with better marketing. The most natural human systems — from the mammoth hunt to the most effective modern teams — are ones where leadership is earned and re-earned, where the chief is the chief because the group respects them, and where someone who stops earning that respect makes way for someone who will.

Network marketing teaches you to build people. The Contribution Framework rewards you for building and for strengthening what others have built before you build something of your own. Together, they produce people who can do both — and who lead through development rather than control. That is the kind of person who is ready to start a subsidiary. That is the kind of person this system needs.

### The Dividend Bridge

There is an elegant symmetry between the two systems that is worth stating explicitly, because it is by design.

Class B dividends are strongest in your early years, when you are actively contributing to a subsidiary and minting shares every quarter. Network marketing income is weakest in those same early years, when the business is still being built and the network has not yet reached the scale where it compounds on its own. As time passes and you step back from active subsidiary work, your shares naturally dilute and your dividend income gradually fades. But by then, if you have been building, the network marketing business is doing the opposite — maturing, self-replicating, compounding without your direct involvement.

One is designed to decay without active involvement. The other is designed to grow without it. They cross in the middle. If you have built both, you never have a gap. This allows your Bucket One to always be full.

### Two Streams, Two Jobs

These two income streams are not interchangeable, and they are not both meant to pile into the same bucket. They have separate purposes by design:

**Network marketing income → Bucket One (the safety net).** This is its permanent home. As the network matures and compounds, the overrides flow into land, index funds, and stable long-term assets. This bucket grows quietly, is never touched for lifestyle, and exists so that no matter what happens, the family is covered.

**Dividends and profit share → Bucket Two (active lifestyle and runway).** This is what funds your life. It covers your expenses, gives you room to reinvest, and — critically — provides the runway you need while building a subsidiary in its unprofitable early years. A new business may take years to become meaningfully profitable. Without Bucket Two income, those early years put you into survival mode. With it, you can think long-term and actually build rather than scrambling to keep the lights on.

In the early phase, while you are still working inside a subsidiary and your Layer 2 pay covers daily life, both streams may be going into reinvestment — minting more shares with profit share, feeding early network marketing earnings into Bucket One. That is fine. The structure is flexible in the early days. But the mature design is two streams, two buckets, two purposes. Network marketing builds the floor under your feet. Dividends and profit share keep the engine running while you build. When your subsidiary starts generating its own income, that takes over Bucket Two, and the dividends can go wherever you choose — reinvest, mint more shares, or redirect alongside your network marketing income into Bucket One.

### The Accessible Path to Wealth

This is where the full vision comes together. Traditionally, network marketing requires you to work a traditional job while building on the side. That job drains your energy, pays you the same whether you hustle or coast, and gives you no ownership over anything. Faleth Capital replaces that job with something fundamentally better: contribution-based work that pays you for output, does not track your hours, generates profit share you can mint into appreciating equity, and explicitly gives you the freedom to build on the side.

The idea is to start in both systems simultaneously. Build both. By the time one is generating a full-time income, the other should be as well. Once both are producing full-time income, you are ready to start a subsidiary. Your network marketing income covers Bucket One, securing your family regardless of what happens. Your dividends and profit share cover Bucket Two, funding your lifestyle while the subsidiary finds its footing. You are not betting everything on an unproven business. You are building from a position of strength, with two independent income streams already in place, each doing a different job.

This is the path to wealth accessible to just about anyone. It does not mean everyone will walk it. But it lowers the barrier further than any single model can on its own. Network marketing stops being just a "quit your job" side hustle and becomes what it works best as: your true business education and your entry into wealth. And the Contribution Framework stops being just a better paycheck and becomes what it is designed to be: the launchpad for ownership.

## 1.6 — The Pipeline

Faleth Capital is not just a holding company. It is a pathway. The system is designed so that a person can:

1. Join any subsidiary at any level and earn contribution-based pay (Layer 2: weekly value share).
2. Build wealth through two layers of quarterly profit sharing — one from their subsidiary, one from the family office — with the option to mint equity shares at each.
3. Use the freedom of a no-time-clock model to build a network marketing business or other venture on the side, developing entrepreneurial skills.
4. When ready, start their own business funded and owned by Faleth Capital, mentored by existing office members.
5. Once that business generates $1 million per year in collected income for the office, earn Class A membership and a seat at the table.
6. As a member, mentor the next generation of business builders for a share of the mentoring fees, completing the cycle.
7. As personal wealth accumulates, have the same family office manage it through the Class C managed capital structure — the same team, the same philosophy, the same structural safeguards, without needing to find an outside wealth manager.

This is not theoretical. It is the structural intention of every mechanism described in this document. Every rule, every incentive, every allocation is designed to move people along this pipeline from contributor to owner to mentor to managed wealth. The system does not just help people build wealth. It manages it once they have it. The same office that helped you earn it can steward it for the long term.

## 1.7 — The Spine of the System

Everything described in this constitution — the Contribution Framework, the equity model, the two buckets, the pipeline — was designed with a specific structural relationship in mind. It is important to state it explicitly, because it shapes how the whole system should be understood.

**Network marketing is the spine of Faleth Capital. Faleth Capital is the multiplier.**

This is not a casual observation. It is the architectural reality.

Network marketing — specifically, a well-built network marketing business developed to the point of genuine full-time passive income — is the only income model that can become genuinely, structurally passive in a way that is accessible to nearly anyone willing to do the work. It is not passive at first. It requires years of development, genuine investment in other people, building them to the point they dont need you anymore, and the discipline to be okay with not being needed by them. But when it is built correctly, it generates income that compounds without your daily involvement, flows into Bucket One indefinitely, and can be passed to the next generation. Nothing else in this system does that with the same permanence.

Faleth Capital was designed, in large part, to be the best possible environment from which to build a network marketing business. The traditional alternative — working a job while building on the side — drains your energy, pays you the same whether you perform or not, and gives you no ownership over anything. Faleth Capital replaces that with something structurally superior: contribution-based pay that rewards your output, a no-time-clock model that gives you genuine freedom, profit sharing that builds equity, and a culture that actively supports entrepreneurial development on the side. You are not building your network marketing business in the cracks of a system that resents you for it. You are building it inside a system designed to make that possible.

At the same time, network marketing has real structural limits. You do not control the product. You cannot pivot toward a market gap you identify. The hierarchy is fixed, which is powerful in some respects — your downline is your downline permanently, and their success is always your success — but it means the compensation structure is not built to reward every kind of value creation. It rewards network expansion and network development. It does not reward the person who walks into an existing organization and makes it dramatically more efficient, or who identifies a product the market needs and figures out how to build it.

Faleth Capital fills exactly those gaps. It rewards both network building and network strengthening through the Contribution Framework. It gives participants a path to genuine ownership of real businesses. It provides the capital, mentorship, and structure to turn entrepreneurial capability — developed in the network marketing dojo — into enterprises that can scale beyond what any single distributor network can reach.

**The relationship is complementary, not competitive.** Network marketing does what Faleth Capital cannot: build a truly permanent, compounding, passive income stream accessible to anyone willing to develop people. Faleth Capital does what network marketing cannot: reward the full range of value creation, provide genuine equity, and build real operating businesses that the participant actually owns.

The ideal participant is building both simultaneously. The network marketing business is feeding Bucket One, teaching leadership, and providing the permanent safety net. Faleth Capital is providing the active income, the equity, the business education, and eventually the platform to build a subsidiary. By the time someone is ready to start their own business under Faleth Capital, they should have both engines running — not because the system requires it, but because the person who has both is starting from a position of genuine strength rather than hope.

### The Discipleship Pattern

Network marketing, at its best, is a discipleship pattern with incentives. You invest in people. You develop them past the point of needing you. You celebrate when they surpass you. You remain connected to them permanently, and their growth continues to benefit you in perpetuity — not because the contract requires it, but because you built something real together.

This is not a coincidence. The most durable human development systems have always operated on this pattern: a more experienced person pours into a less experienced one, transfers genuine capability, and then gets out of the way. The student eventually teaches. The teacher's legacy compounds through every person the student develops. The system is self-replicating because the incentive to develop others is built into the structure itself.

Faleth Capital is designed around the same logic. The mentoring fee structure — where Class A members earn a percentage of the fees from subsidiaries they help build — creates the same dynamic. The mentor wins when the mentee wins. The incentive to develop people past the point of needing you is structural, not aspirational. And the Contribution Framework creates the same self-organizing dynamic at the team level: the Elected Lead earns more when the team produces more, which means the most natural move is always to make the team more capable, not more dependent.

What network marketing teaches in the sales and leadership context, Faleth Capital applies across every function of a business. The discipleship pattern is the same. The arena is larger.

## 1.8 — The Five-Step Blueprint

The pipeline described in Section 1.6 outlines the general arc from contributor to owner to mentor. This section makes that arc more tactical for the individual who is starting from zero and wants to know exactly what they are building toward.

The five steps below represent the ideal sequence. They are not mandatory prescriptions — the system is flexible enough to accommodate different starting points and different goals. But they represent the clearest path from nothing to the full expression of what this system is designed to produce.

**Step One: Get Started (Network Marketing)**
Begin building a network marketing business. Buy your own products. Use them. Build a genuine testimonial. This is not about income yet — it is about beginning the education. You are learning what it means to build something without authority, without a paycheck to offer, and without anything to lean on except your own development and the value you create for others. Start here regardless of where you are in life. The education begins the moment you do.

**Step Two: Maximize Active Income**
Whether you are working a traditional job, contributing inside a Faleth Capital subsidiary, or running your own small operation, maximize your active income. This is the engine that covers your life while Step One compounds in the background. If you are inside a Faleth Capital subsidiary, your Layer 2 pay is your active income. Your profit share and dividends are building equity. Your network marketing business is building in the background on your own time, on your own terms, without anyone's permission.

**Step Three: Build the Network Marketing Business to Full-Time Income**
The goal at this step is to reach the full-time income threshold in your network marketing business — the point where the business is generating genuine passive income that flows directly into Bucket One. This is not just an income goal. It is a proof-of-concept that you can develop people to independence. Reaching full-time income means you have built and developed real leaders. It means your financial safety net is now self-funding. It means you have learned the core leadership skill this entire system is built on: how to make yourself unnecessary to the people you develop.

Note that true passivity — the ability to fully step away — typically requires deeper development than the initial full-time income milestone. That first threshold proves the skill. Real depth means you have built multiple legs of leaders who are themselves developing leaders, each with teams that produce without their daily involvement. The deeper you build, the more durable the passivity. The first full-time income milestone is the proof of concept. The levels beyond it are where it becomes structurally permanent.

**Step Four: Maximize Active Income Again — Through Your Own Business**
With Bucket One now generating passive income and the leadership skills developed through Step Three, you are ready to build something in the marketplace. This may be a Faleth Capital subsidiary, or it may be your own independent operation. The goal is the same: generate meaningful active income from a business you own and operate. This is Bucket Two at full expression — not a job, not a side hustle, but a real business generating real returns. The skills you built in Step Three translate directly here: you know how to develop people without negative reinforcement. Now you have a business context in which to apply that.

**Step Five: Grow Both Simultaneously — Indefinitely**
This is not a destination. It is a permanent operating posture. The network marketing business keeps compounding into Bucket One. The business keeps growing. The larger the business becomes, the more it demands the same leadership skills that network marketing keeps sharpening. Network marketing remains the dojo — the place where you keep learning to develop leaders of leaders in the most demanding possible environment — while the business is where you apply those skills at scale and generate the wealth that the system is designed to produce.

By the time someone is operating at full expression of Step Five, they have two independent income streams, two full buckets, a proven track record of developing people, and a business that runs on genuine alignment rather than fear. They are not building from desperation. They are not relying on any single system. They are exactly the kind of person this system was designed to produce.

# Article II: Structure

## 2.1 — The Family Office

Faleth Capital is the parent holding entity. It owns all subsidiaries. It maintains the shared liquidity pool, manages the equity (share) system, collects fees from subsidiaries, distributes profit shares and dividends, and serves as the governance body for the portfolio.

The office itself is designed to have minimal overhead. It is a governing body over a shared pool of capital, not an operating business. Its income comes from subsidiary fees. Its expenses should be negligible relative to the portfolio's revenue.

## 2.2 — Subsidiaries

Each subsidiary is technically owned by Faleth Capital but practically operated by its owner. Subsidiaries have free reign to run their business as if they were independent, with full autonomy over operations, hiring, pricing, strategy, and culture. The only structural requirements are:

- Adherence to the [[The Contribution Framework|Contribution Framework]] for compensation (no wages or salaries).
- Payment of the parent company fee (see [[Faleth Capital/The Financial Framework]]).
- Quarterly financial reporting to the office for consolidated profit calculations.
- Participation in the shared equity system (see [[The Equity Framework]]).

Beyond these requirements, each subsidiary governs itself. The office provides mentorship and resources — capital, operational guidance, shared vendor relationships, and the collective knowledge of its members — but does not impose operational mandates. The philosophy is simple: the people closest to the work know best how to do the work.

## 2.3 — The Contribution Framework

Every subsidiary operates under the [[The Contribution Framework]], a process-based compensation model that replaces wages and salaries with contribution points, a gross-margin-funded team pool, a guaranteed floor, and layered quarterly profit sharing. Full mechanics — the point system, payout formulas, the Elected Lead structure, and subsidiary-level governance — are covered there.

# Article III: Financial Structure

The financial architecture of Faleth Capital is designed around one principle: every dollar flows through a transparent, calculable system where every participant can trace the path from revenue to their own earnings. No black boxes. No discretionary allocations. The math is simple enough that a fifth grader could audit it.

The system operates at two levels. At the subsidiary level, revenue flows through cost of goods, the team pool (Cost of Fulfillment), operating expenses, and the parent company fee, with 20% of net profit distributed quarterly as a local profit share. At the office level, 20% of total portfolio balance sheet growth is distributed as profit share to all participants, 30% of the office's own holdings growth flows to Class B shareholders as dividends, and 50% is retained in the liquidity pool.

All quarterly allocations are calculated simultaneously off the same balance sheet snapshot. No calculation's output feeds into another's input. The simplicity is deliberate, because simplicity is what makes transparency real rather than aspirational.

The office maintains a liquidity reserve equivalent to the combined balance sheets of all its subsidiaries — the Talmudic one-third principle applied as policy. This ensures capital is always available to fund the next builder, weather downturns, and seize opportunities without external financing.

Full mechanics — the parent company fee structure, subsidiary and office-level financial flows, the 20/30/50 allocation, layered incentive alignment, structural redundancy, and the liquidity reserve policy — are detailed in [[Faleth Capital/The Financial Framework]].

# Article IV: The Equity System

Faleth Capital uses a mint-and-burn equity model. Shares are created when participants invest their earned profit share or dividends, and destroyed when they cash out at book value. There is no fixed pool to exhaust, no buyback negotiation, and no outside capital can purchase internal shares. Every share in existence represents earned contribution that someone chose to reinvest.

The system has three share classes. Class A shares represent office membership — full ownership, voting rights, and stewardship of the firm, earned by building a subsidiary to the $1M income threshold. Class B shares are non-voting, dividend-participating equity available to all participants, minted with profit share and burned on cash-out at book value. Class C shares represent externally managed capital — a pooled investment fund where outside investors gain exposure to the portfolio under standard PE terms (2 and 20), entirely separate from the office's own dividend and profit structures.

The key principles: Class B book value is always assets minus liabilities divided by shares outstanding — no subjective multiples, no discretionary inputs. Anyone can cash out at any time at book value. Class C capital nets to zero on the office's balance sheet and does not inflate Class B share prices. And the dilution factor — whether dividend pool growth outpaced share count growth — is reported every quarter so everyone can see the health of their investment.

Full mechanics — minting and burning procedures, share class rights and transferability, how Class C interacts with the existing system, the investor proposition, worked examples, and parameters still to be determined — are detailed in [[The Equity Framework]].

# Article V: Membership

Class A membership in Faleth Capital is earned, not granted. The pathway runs from participant to subsidiary founder to office member: demonstrate capability, propose a business, build it under Faleth Capital's funding and mentorship, and earn your seat when the subsidiary generates $1 million per year in collected income for the office.

Membership, once earned, is permanent — it represents recognition of what someone built. But active governance is a responsibility that requires the ongoing confidence of one's peers, the same principle that applies to Elected Leads within subsidiaries. A member can be voted out of active decision-making by the other Class A members without losing their shares or economic interests.

Members are expected to participate in quarterly governance, mentor aspiring builders, operate their subsidiary under the [[The Contribution Framework|Contribution Framework]], maintain transparent financials, and act in the long-term interest of the portfolio. Mentoring is directly incentivized: mentors receive 20% of the parent company fees collected from the subsidiaries they help build.

Full mechanics — the path to membership, permanence and accountability provisions, member responsibilities, and mentoring compensation — are detailed in [[The Membership Framework]].

# Article VI: Governance

Faleth Capital operates as a confederacy of sovereign subsidiaries united by shared principles, shared capital, and shared incentives. Governance reflects this: each subsidiary governs itself, and the office governs the portfolio.

At the subsidiary level, the team collectively determines its own [[The Contribution Framework|Contribution Framework]] parameters — point values, COF rate, floor amount, profit share percentage — and elects its own Lead. The office does not interfere with subsidiary operations unless the subsidiary is in violation of this constitution.

At the office level, decisions are made by Class A members through simple majority vote, one member one vote, regardless of subsidiary size. These decisions include approving new subsidiaries, amending this constitution, adjusting the parent company fee or allocation splits, admitting new members, and removing members from active governance.

Faleth Capital operates with radical transparency. The consolidated balance sheet, dilution factor, book value per share, total share count, and subsidiary performance summaries are open to all participants quarterly. The balance sheet is the single source of truth for share valuation. Clean books are non-negotiable.

Office-level governance procedures are intentionally minimal in this founding stage. As the membership grows, the founding Class A members will formalize additional procedures based on real operating experience. The principles are established here. The processes will be shaped by the people who live them.

Full mechanics — office-level decision-making, subsidiary-level self-governance, transparency requirements, and the quarterly earnings call format — are detailed in [[The Governance Framework]].

# Article VII: Separation and the Peaceful Fork

Faleth Capital is built on voluntary participation. The same principles that make the system work — aligned incentives, genuine ownership, freedom to contribute on your own terms — also require that people can leave on their own terms. A system that traps people creates exactly the resentment it was designed to prevent.

This is not a concession. It is a structural commitment. The availability of a graceful exit is what makes every day someone stays a genuine vote of confidence in the system. People who can leave freely tend to work harder at staying.

The system rests on three inviolable principles: **no trapping** — no participant or group may be forced to remain against their will; **book value as the standard** — all separations use transparent assets-minus-liabilities valuation, with no subjective multiples or punitive discounts; and **protection of earned stakes** — every participant keeps what they built, including contribution history, Class B shares, and proportional equity claims.

When a group no longer wishes to operate under Faleth Capital, the Peaceful Fork provides a structured path for separation. The team — not the office, not the founder — drives the process. Those who stay retain the existing subsidiary. Those who leave may form an independent entity with a prorated share of assets at book value. The office's role is narrow: ensure the process honors the principles, and protect the liquidity reserve for those who remain.

The specific terms of any fork — thresholds, timelines, transition agreements — are negotiated in good faith at the time of separation. The principles are fixed. The process is flexible.

Full mechanics — the detailed shape of a fork, the office's facilitation role, and review procedures — are detailed in [[The Separation Framework]].

# Article VIII: Acquisitions and the Transition Commitment

Faleth Capital does not only build businesses from scratch. When an existing business and its owner align with the mission, the family office can acquire that business and bring it into the portfolio. But an acquisition is not a founding. It has people who did not choose this system. Handling that transition with integrity is not optional — it is a defining test of whether we mean what we say.

The governing commitments are: **no surprises** — every person learns what is happening before anything changes; **compensation is honored** — existing pay becomes the non-credited Minimum Pay Guarantee during transition; **observation before decision** — every participant sees the [[The Contribution Framework|Contribution Framework]] operating in a live subsidiary before being asked to work under it; **voluntary participation** — anyone who opts out receives a generous severance and departs with dignity; **phased transition** — the business transitions in stages, with early departments serving as proof of concept for those still waiting; and **the business keeps running** — operational continuity is non-negotiable.

An acquired business is not a conquered territory. It is a group of people being invited into a better system. The invitation must be genuine, or it is not an invitation at all.

Full mechanics — the transition sequence, the observation and opt-out process, department-by-department rollout, the middle management question, and the specific commitments — are detailed in [[The Acquisition Transition Framework]].

# Article IX: Amendments

This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of active Class A members. Proposed amendments must be distributed to all Class A members at least 30 days before the vote. Amendments take effect at the start of the following quarter.

The following provisions may not be amended:

- The prohibition on wages and salaries within subsidiaries (Article II, Section 2.3).
- The requirement that Class B shares can only be acquired through earned profit share or dividends (see [[The Equity Framework]]).
- The transparency requirements (Article VI; see [[The Governance Framework]]).
- The mint-and-burn mechanism for Class B shares (see [[The Equity Framework]]).
- The right of any Class B shareholder to cash out at book value at any time (see [[The Equity Framework]]).
- The liquidity reserve policy requiring liquid capital equivalent to combined subsidiary balance sheets (see [[Faleth Capital/The Financial Framework]]).
- The three core principles of the Separation and Peaceful Fork mechanism: no trapping, the book-value valuation standard, and the protection of earned stakes (Article VII; see [[The Separation Framework]]).

These provisions are foundational to the identity and integrity of Faleth Capital. They represent commitments to every person who participates in the system. They are not subject to change by majority vote.

# Article X: What This Is For

This is not a corporate charter designed to protect shareholders from liability. Lawyers will handle that paperwork separately. This is something different.

This is the document that says what we believe, how we operate, and what we will not compromise on. It is the thing we hand someone when they ask what Faleth Capital actually is. It is the thing we refer back to when we disagree about direction. It is the thing that keeps us honest when it would be easier or more profitable in the short term to cut corners.

The wage and salary model has had centuries to prove itself. It has produced a world where most people spend the majority of their waking hours doing work they do not care about, for compensation that does not reflect their contribution, under conditions that do not respect their autonomy. We believe that is not a law of nature. It is a design flaw. And design flaws can be fixed.

This document is our fix. It is a draft. It will evolve. But the principles will not.

One thing worth noting: the layers of this system — contribution-based pay, subsidiary profit sharing, office-level profit sharing, dividends, network marketing, the two buckets — were each designed to solve a specific, separate problem. None of them were designed as backups for the others. But because each was built honestly, they naturally create redundancy. The safety in this system was not bolted on. It emerged from the structure itself. We take that as evidence that the architecture is right.

There is one more thing worth saying, and it would be dishonest not to name it directly.

The purpose of this organization is to be a reflection of the love that Christ has shown to me. That is what is at the core of what drives me, what ultimately drives this company, and what drove this system to exist in the first place.

You do not have to believe what I believe to participate in this system. The math works regardless of your worldview. The incentives are real regardless of your convictions. This is an inclusive system — anyone can get out of it whatever they want, and nobody will be corralled in one direction or another. But I believe that those who have eyes to see and ears to hear will come to see what I have seen. And it would be dishonest of me to hide that.

The principles in this document are not just good business philosophy. They come from what I believe to be good life philosophy. I believe that if you help enough other people make their dreams come true, yours will come true by default.

So if you have read this far, know this: I love you. I believe in you. And if you are willing to let me and this organization into your life, I can promise we will do everything in our power to make your dreams come true — as long as you remain fighting for them, and fight for the dreams of others in return.

Thank you, and God bless.

_The principles are permanent. The parameters are not. This constitution evolves through the collective judgment of its members, within the boundaries of what cannot be changed._
