## Business Model

Free Range Repair is a device restoration business. The core philosophy is that customers don't want a repair — they want their device restored to its pre-damaged state. The business offers three tiers:

| Offer | Price (relative to new device) |
|---|---|
| Refurbished Device | ~75% of new price |
| Restoration (board swap) | ~50% of new price |
| DIY Parts & Tools | Varies |

**Example:** If a new device costs $800 → Refurb at $600, Restoration at $400.

The restoration process involves swapping boards between the customer's device and a known-working refurbished unit, so the customer receives a functionally new device in their original housing.

**LCD Buyback:** If the screen is intact after a repair, the shop can recover $20–$100 by selling it back through a buyback program.

## Upsell Offers

Offered on every repair at point of checkout.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Screen Protector | $20 |
| Case | $40 |
| Both (bundled) | $50 (saves $10) |

Each upsell item also grants free insurance months (see Insurance Plan below).

## Insurance Plan

A recurring monthly plan ($20/month) providing full coverage on either a restoration or refurbished device.

### Free Insurance Earned at Checkout

| Purchase | Free Insurance Months |
|---|---|
| Repair only | 2 months |
| + Screen protector | +2 months (4 total) |
| + Case | +2 months (4 total) |
| + Both (case & screen protector) | +4 months (6 total) |

The upsell items function as a sample of the insurance product, designed to convert one-time customers into recurring subscribers.

## Referral Program

Point-based referral system delivered via a two-sided personalized business card.

### Referrer Rewards (cumulative points)

| Points | Reward |
|---|---|
| 1 point | Free screen protector + 2 months insurance |
| 2 points | Free case & screen protector + 4 months insurance |
| 3 points | Free restoration + case + screen protector + 6 months insurance |

No cap on points a referrer can accumulate.

### Referred Customer Reward

Free screen protector + 4 months insurance on their first restoration.

### Business Card Design

- **Referred side:** QR code with referee's code, customer name, and what they receive by redeeming
- **Referrer side:** Referral reward tiers listed as a reminder of what they earn by handing cards out

## Compensation Plan

Work is broken into individual processes, each assigned a point value. Associates fulfill processes based on their strengths and preferences — no fixed roles.

**Payout formula:**

> Individual Points ÷ Total Points on Project = Share of COF Payout

Fewer total processes on a project = each point is worth more.

### Quarterly Profit Share

Distributed quarterly. Each associate's share is determined by:

> Individual Cumulative Earnings ÷ Total Team Earnings = Share of Profit Pool

The profit share scales with shop performance — the more the shop earns, the more everyone earns.

For full process breakdowns and point value examples, see `Comp Plan Examples.xlsx` → "Electronics Repair" tab. See also [[The Contribution Framework]] for the full governance and financial model behind this system.

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Total | Full cost to customer including taxes and fees |
| Subtotal | Cost to customer before taxes and fees |
| Gross Profit | Subtotal − COG |
| COG | Cost of Goods |
| COA | Cost of Acquisition (equivalent to CAC) |
| COF | Cost of Fulfillment — the labor pool set-aside percentage |
| Fixed Costs | Guaranteed recurring monthly expenses |
| PCB | Project Completion Bonus — paid when project is completed |
| PC Royalties | Royalties paid to the parent company (Faleth Capital) |
